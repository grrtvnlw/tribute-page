Steve Jobs

"I want to put a ding in the universe."

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, CA on February 24.
  • 1961 - The Jobs family moves to Mountain View, CA.
  • 1968 - Steve Jobs gets an internship at Hewlett-Packard.
  • 1970 - Jobs meets future Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak through a friend.
  • 1972 - Graduates from Homestead High School in Cupertino, CA and enrolls at Reed College in Portland, OR.
  • 1974 - Jobs begins a brief stint as an engineer at Atari, working on a single-player version of Pong.
  • 1976 - Along with Wozniak and Ronald Wayne co-founds Apple Computer and sells the Apple I for $666.66.
  • 1977 - Apple incorporates and releases the Apple II.
  • 1980 - Apple goes public; Jobs' net worth above $200 million.
  • 1980 - Apple introduces the MacIntosh, the first commercially successful personal computer with a graphical user interface.
  • 1985 - Jobs resigns as Apple chairman and starts NeXT Computer.
  • 1986 - Jobs buys the Graphics Group division of Lucasfilm for $10 million which later becomes Pixar Animation Studios.
  • 1988 - NeXT releases its first computer.
  • 1995 - Jobs becomes Pixar's president and CEO.
  • 1996 - Jobs returns to Apple, as an advisor, after Apple buys NeXT for $429 million.
  • 1997 - Becomes interim CEO of Apple and announces a $150 million investment from Microsoft, as well as a partnership on Microsoft Office and Internet Explorer for the Mac.
  • 1997 - Introduces the Apple Store, which lets consumers custom-order Apple products directly from the company online.
  • 1998 - Apple introduces and releases the iMac.
  • 2000 - Jobs drops the "interim" from his CEO title; takes a $1 annual salary.
  • 2001 - Apple introduces iTunes, Mac OS X Cheetah, the iPod, and opens first retail stores in Tysons Corner, VA and Glendale, CA.
  • 2003 - Apple opens iTunes Music Store.
  • 2004 - Jobs undergoes surgery to remove a cancerous tumor in his pancreas.
  • 2005 - Apple introduces iPod Shuffle, iPod Nano, and iPod with video. Also announces a switch from IBM chips to Intel's Core Duo chips in new MacBook Pro and iMac.
  • 2006 - Apple's market capitalization surpasses Dell's; Disney buys Pixar for $7.4 billion, Jobs' net worth estimated to be $8.3 billion.
  • 2007 - Apple introduces iPhone and iPod Touch.
  • 2008 - Apple introduces the App Store, MacBook Air, and MobileMe, Apple's first cloud offering.
  • 2009 - Jobs takes 6-month leave of absence for medical reasons.
  • 2010 - Apple introduces iPad.
  • 2010 - Jobs convinces Apple Corps. and EMI Group to make Beatles' albums available on iTunes.
  • 2011 - Jobs goes on medical leave of absence from CEO post; gives keynote speech at Apple Worldwide Developers Conference, his last public appearance at an Apple event.
  • 2011 - Jobs pitches Cupertino, CA on a new Apple campus. Apple shares edge past those of Exxon Mobile to become the most valuable U.S. companty; market capitalization of $337.17 billion.
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs passes away on October 5 in Palo Alto, CA. RIP and thank you, Steve.
